Rough Notes from DMBOK Study (Data Governance and Data Management)

Published on 2021/11/09 📖 1 min read
DMBOK

These are rough notes from my DMBOK study, focusing on Data Governance and Data Management.

  • What is the DAMA Wheel?

    • A framework that summarizes the disciplines needed to perform data governance

  • What is Data Governance?

    • Definition: Exercising control over data asset management through authority and accountability
      • Proper organizational staffing
        • Building organizational structures
          • Data stewardship system
          • In-house critical areas while outsourcing routine operations, etc.
      • Executive commitment is required
      • Sharing of data management activities
      • Starting small is recommended
      • Regular sharing of activity scope
    • Goal: The organization manages its own data as an asset
      • Define, approve, communicate, and enforce principles, policies, procedures, metrics, tools, and responsibilities related to data management
      • Monitor and guide policy compliance, data usage, and management activities
    • Note that data governance is a means, not an end. Avoid management for the sake of management.

  • Direction Data Governance Should Aim For

    • Data should be managed as an enterprise asset
    • Best practices in data management should be promoted across the organization
    • Enterprise data strategy should directly align with overall business strategy
    • Data management processes should be continuously improved

  • ISO 55000/55001

    • International standards related to asset management

    • To ensure maximum availability and profitability while balancing cost, risk, and performance throughout the lifecycle of organizational assets

      Japan Asset Management Association (JAAM) | What is the ISO 55000 Series? https://www.ja-am.or.jp/iso55000s

      Asset Management System (AMS) Certification (ISO 55001) | Japan Accreditation Board https://www.jab.or.jp/iso/iso_55001/

  • Approaches to Data Valuation

    • Cost of data acquisition and storage
    • Cost of data recovery if data is lost
    • Clarification of the purpose of selling data
    • Expected revenue from data usage

  • Objectives of Data Management

    • To enable effective use of data to add value to the enterprise
    • Risk mitigation
    • Process improvement
      • Regulatory compliance
      • Data quality improvement
      • Metadata management
      • Development project efficiency
      • Vendor management

  • Data Steward

    • Role

      • A business role appointed to be responsible for the quality and usage of an organization’s data assets

    • Examples of Duties

      • Creating and managing metadata
      • Documenting rules and standards
      • Managing data quality issues
      • Executing data governance operational activities

    • Various types of data stewards exist

    • Chief Data Steward: Executive sponsor

    • Executive Data Steward: Senior manager who serves on the data governance council

    • Enterprise Data Steward: Covers data domains across business functions

    • Business Data Steward: Defines and controls data in cooperation with stakeholders

    • Data Owner: Also serves as a business data steward, with authority to approve decisions regarding data within their area of responsibility

    • IT Data Steward: IT professional who functions in each knowledge area, such as data integration specialist, database administrator, business intelligence specialist, data quality analyst, and metadata administrator

    • Coordinating Data Steward: Represents both business data steward and IT data steward teams, engaging in discussions between teams and with executive data stewards

  • What is Data Governance?

    • A continuous process for data improvement
    • Various organizational models are possible
    • Exercise of authority and control over data asset management
    • Monitor and verify compliance with regulatory and compliance requirements

  • Who is Responsible for Executing Data Governance?

    • Everyone in the data management community
      • Data stewards
      • CEO/CIO/CDO
      • Business stakeholders

  • Data Asset Valuation The process of understanding and measuring the economic value of data in an organization. Value is created when the economic benefits of using data exceed the costs of acquiring and storing data and the costs of managing risks associated with its use.

  • Example Approaches for Delivering the Value of Data Governance

    • Maintaining an intranet website for data management
    • Publishing regular newsletters
    • Encouraging participation in forums and communities
    • Creating elevator pitches for stakeholders and audiences

  • Content to Include on the Data Management Intranet Website

    • Contact information for the data governance organization
    • Executive messages about data management and existing data management issues
    • Profiles of data steward teams
    • Mechanisms and email links for reporting data management issues

  • Data Standards Guidelines

    • Regular reviews and updates are required

  • Data Governance Steering Committee

    • The primary and highest-level body responsible for overseeing and supporting data governance activities

  • ISO 3166

    • Geocoding assigned by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) for country names, corresponding areas, and regions such as prefectures and states
    • ISO 3166 - Wikipedia https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_3166

  • Objectives of Data Management

    • Acquisition, storage, protection, and ensuring integrity of data assets
    • Ensuring the quality of data and information
    • Ensuring the privacy and confidentiality of stakeholder data

  • Data Governance Strategy

    • Must be linked to the overall business strategy, not just the IT strategy
      • Operations framework and accountability
      • Implementation roadmap
      • Operational plan
      • Statement of the significance, vision, mission, and principles of data governance
    • Support for data management projects
      • Involvement and alignment with internal SDLC, service deployment management, ITIL, and PMO processes is required
    • Establishing collaboration points with the organization
      • Consider collaboration structures with organizations that partner on data governance activities
        • Procurement and contracts
        • Budget and funding
        • Regulatory compliance
        • SDLC/development frameworks
    • Data Management Maturity
      • Measure the current data management execution capability and scope
        • Transformation capability
        • Readiness for collaboration
        • Alignment with business
