AWS Certified Database – Specialty (DBS-C01) Exam Experience

Published on 2020/10/28 📖 1 min read
AWS

It was a while ago, but I passed the AWS Certified Database - Specialty exam. Since I do a lot of database-related work, it’s great to have certifications that validate my knowledge and skills. This brings my database certifications to three, alongside Oracle Master Gold and OSS-DB Gold.

This certification was released in May 2020, and not much time had passed since then. I hadn’t published my notes from that time as a blog post, so I’m writing up my study methods in hopes it will be helpful for those planning to take the exam.

Exam Overview

https://aws.amazon.com/jp/certification/certified-database-specialty/

  • Duration: 180 minutes
  • Number of questions: 65
  • Passing score: 75% or higher

Study Methods

1. Start by skimming the exam guide

https://d1.awsstatic.com/ja_JP/training-and-certification/docs-database-specialty/AWS-Certified-Database-Specialty_Exam-Guide.pdf

2. Study the BlackBelt materials for the following services

Rather than reading the PDF materials, I recommend watching on YouTube with subtitles at 2x speed. I feel that absorbing information through both eyes and ears improves retention.

High Importance
  • Relational Database Service (RDS)
  • Aurora with MySQL Compatibility
  • Aurora with PostgreSQL Compatibility
  • DynamoDB
  • CloudFormation
  • Key Management Service
Medium to Low Importance
  • ElastiCache
  • DocumentDB
  • Neptune
  • Redshift
3. Free e-learning course for AWS Certified Database - Specialty exam preparation

Lectures are provided for each section listed in the exam guide.

https://www.aws.training/Details/eLearning?id=47245

4. Understand the question patterns

https://devopspages.io/aws-certified-database-specialty-exam-guide/

http://drawing-hisa.seesaa.net/article/473054025.html

5. Get hands-on experience extensively

I worked through nearly all Aurora, RDS, and DynamoDB features based on the documentation. Topics like Aurora Global Database, Aurora Serverless, Aurora Cloning, and auditing — features you might think you’d never actually use — do appear on the exam, so I recommend focusing heavily on these services.

6. Purchase and work through paid practice exams

I used the following practice exam set. I found it helpful for getting familiar with the exam questions and patterns, though I felt the number of DynamoDB questions was a bit high.

https://www.whizlabs.com/learn/course/aws-certified-database-specialty/

7. Take the practice exam

A practice exam is also available for this certification, so I took it as a final check before the actual exam. I scored 60% on the practice exam and thought I was in trouble!

Question Distribution

Here’s what the question distribution felt like when I took the exam:

No Service Weight
1 RDS/Aurora 40%
2 DynamoDB 15%
3 DMS/SCT 15%
4 Key Management Service 5%
5 Redshift 5%
5 Troubleshooting 5%
6 Others 15%

Final Thoughts

This was my first AWS Specialty certification, so I was really happy when I passed. That said, I didn’t find it particularly difficult, and I don’t think earning this Specialty certification necessarily proves deep expertise — it’s more like demonstrating a general knowledge across the field. Someone once said that a certification is just the first step in a domain, and I completely agree with that.
